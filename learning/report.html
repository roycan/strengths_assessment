    About the VARK Model

    What is VARK?

    VARK stands for Visual, Auditory, Read/Write, and Kinesthetic learning preferences. The model was developed by Neil Fleming and Colleen Mills in 1992 as a practical framework to help students understand how they prefer to take in and process information.

    The Research Foundation

    The VARK model builds on earlier work in sensory modalities and learning styles. Fleming's key insight was that students could benefit from understanding their preferences and actively choosing study strategies that align with those preferences.

    Fleming, N. D., & Mills, C. (1992). Not Another Inventory, Rather a Catalyst for Reflection. To Improve the Academy, 11, 137-155.

    Multi-Modal Learners

    Research shows that 60-70% of students are multi-modal learners—they benefit from using strategies from multiple categories. If your scores were close, that's completely normal and often advantageous!

    Important Considerations

    • Preferences vs. Abilities: VARK measures preferences, not intelligence or fixed abilities. You can learn in any modality—this just shows what feels most natural.
    • Context Matters: Your ideal learning approach may vary by subject or situation. Math might need kinesthetic practice, while history might call for more reading.
    • Growth Mindset: Learning styles can evolve. Building skills in less-preferred modalities makes you a more flexible, resilient learner.
